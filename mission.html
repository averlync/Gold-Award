About Me

My name is Averlyn and I’ve been a Girl Scout for about 10 years now. I’m a current Girl Scout Ambassador in 12th grade. I started this project because sleep deprivation is something I experience daily. I would always put off sleep to study for a test, write a paper, or watch TV shows after a long day. Sleep was not a priority of mine and because of that, I always suffered from low energy. I hope that through this website, teenagers will see how important sleep is and start prioritizing it more.

2-Week Sleep Experiment

After presenting my website to 5 high school seniors, I conducted a 2-week experiment with all of them in which they each recorded their sleep, mood, and energy levels daily. In addition, they took a brain activity test before and after to see if sleeping more had any affect on how they performed on reaction time, number memory, verbal memory, and visual memory tasks.


Based on the graph showing the change in sleep, mood, and energy levels, the three are strongly correlated. Although other factors could have affected the participants mood and energy level, overall, as the amount of sleep they got increased, their mood and energy level did as well.

After trying to improve their sleep over a 2-week period, there were also increases in reaction time, number memory, verbal memory, and visual memory.

Although other factors could have caused these changes, there is an overall correlation between an increase in sleep and mood, energy levels, reaction time, and memory. Sleep is important for students so it is essential to try and get 8-10 hours of sleep every night!
