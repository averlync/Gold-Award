Tips to Sleep Better at Night

1. Stay healthy! Keep your immune system strong. Drink lots of water. Eat balanced meals. Unhealthy habits can keep you up and prevent you from getting a good night's sleep.

2. Take care of yourself. Implement a self-care routine to soothe yourself. Create a routine for yourself that prepares you for sleep so you can go to sleep feeling calm and refreshed.

3. Shut off electronics. Electronics emit artificial blue light that can interrupt your sleep-wake cycle. Because you are exposed to light, your body delays the release of melatonin, pushing your sleep-wake cycle even later. Turning off your electronics before sleeping will allow your body to unwind.

4. Try to limit your caffeine intake. Caffeine suppresses the release of melatonin which is the hormone that primarily controls your sleep-wake cycle. Too much caffeine will excite your nervous system, preventing you from relaxing before you sleep.

5. Sleep and wake up at consistent times. Studies have shown that setting a consistent sleep-wake schedule for your body aids long-term sleep quality.
